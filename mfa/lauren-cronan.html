Artist Biography

Lauren Cronan hails from Virginia and currently resides in Hartford, Connecticut. She is a first-year Master of Fine Arts student in Digital Media & Design at the University of Connecticut and a graduate research assistant with the digital humanities lab, Greenhouse Studios.

While pursuing her masters, Lauren intends to study digital culture, learning and advocacy alongside printmaking, new media, UI/UX, web and game design. She enjoys digital humanities projects which intersect with social science and technology.

Through Greenhouse, Lauren assists with design and administrative tasks with the Connecticut Digital Archive (CTDA), a digital preservation repository program, and assists with Sourcery, an archival document delivery app, as well as GLAM Gear which seeks to create an end-to-end, low-cost hardware and open source software workflow for archival digitization projects.

Lauren currently serves as the Mentorship Chair for the Connecticut board of the Professional Association for Design (AIGA) where she’s creating a virtual mentorship program for 2021 and assisting with educational outreach.

Lauren has a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Filmmaking from Virginia Commonwealth University (VCUarts). Her foundation is in filmmaking and photography, drawing, painting and sculpture. Her professional background includes academic and public library experience including service, design, programming and teaching.

Artist Statement

Whether I’m photographing or designing, I aim to convey the deep respect and curiosity I have for my subjects. My goals are to engage, preserve and improve community awareness and access to resources especially among stigmatized and disadvantaged groups. I see art, archives, tech and science as interdisciplinary opportunities for learning, healing and activism to improve human rights and ecological conditions.
